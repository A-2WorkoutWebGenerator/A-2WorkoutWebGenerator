Kinetotherapy

Therapeutic healing through movement

Kinetotherapy is a specialized therapeutic discipline based on human movement, used to improve strength, flexibility, coordination, and function through carefully designed exercises. It's an essential component in rehabilitation after injuries, surgeries, and for managing chronic conditions.

When to Seek Kinetotherapy

  • After injury or surgery
  • For chronic pain management
  • To improve movement limitations
  • For neurological conditions
  • For preventive care
  • For sports performance enhancement

Benefits of Kinetotherapy

  • Reduces pain and inflammation
  • Improves joint mobility and muscle strength
  • Enhances balance and coordination
  • Accelerates recovery after injuries
  • Prevents future injuries
  • Improves overall quality of life

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about kinetotherapy practices and benefits

What is the difference between physiotherapy and kinetotherapy?

While both focus on rehabilitation, physiotherapy is a broader field that includes various treatment modalities like electrotherapy, ultrasound, and manual therapy. Kinetotherapy specifically focuses on movement-based exercises and physical activities as therapeutic interventions.

How long does a typical kinetotherapy program last?

The duration varies based on your condition and goals. Acute injuries might require 4-6 weeks, while chronic conditions may need ongoing management. A typical session lasts 30-60 minutes, with frequency ranging from daily to 2-3 times per week.

Can I practice kinetotherapy exercises at home?

Yes, most kinetotherapy programs include home exercises. After initial assessment and training with a professional, you'll receive a customized routine to practice at home. Regular follow-ups ensure proper technique and progression.

Is kinetotherapy covered by insurance?

Many insurance plans cover kinetotherapy when medically necessary and prescribed by a doctor. Coverage varies by provider and plan. It's recommended to check with your insurance company about specific coverage details and requirements.
