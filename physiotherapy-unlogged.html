Physiotherapy

Evidence-based movement and rehabilitation

Physiotherapy is a healthcare profession focused on the prevention, diagnosis, and treatment of physical impairments, movement dysfunctions, and disabilities. It helps restore function, improve mobility, relieve pain, and prevent further injury through exercise, manual therapy, and education.

When to Seek Physiotherapy

  • After injury or surgery
  • For chronic pain management
  • To improve range of motion
  • For sports-related injuries
  • For balance and coordination issues
  • For neurological conditions

What to Expect

  • Initial assessment and diagnosis
  • Treatment plan development
  • Hands-on therapeutic techniques
  • Customized exercise programs
  • Education on self-management
  • Regular progress evaluations

Important Notice

Always consult with a qualified healthcare professional before starting any new exercise program, especially if you have existing health conditions, injuries, or chronic pain.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about physiotherapy practices and benefits

What is the difference between physiotherapy and chiropractic care?

Physiotherapy focuses on restoring movement and function through exercise, manual therapy, and education. Chiropractors primarily focus on the spine and use adjustments to address misalignments. Physiotherapists typically use a broader range of techniques and focus on long-term rehabilitation and pain management strategies.

How many physiotherapy sessions will I need?

The number of sessions varies based on your condition, its severity, and your individual response to treatment. Acute injuries might require 6-8 sessions over a few weeks, while chronic conditions may need ongoing management. Your physiotherapist will develop a personalized treatment plan and provide an estimate during your initial assessment.

Is physiotherapy painful?

Some techniques may cause temporary discomfort, especially when working on injured or inflamed areas. However, treatment should never cause significant pain. Your physiotherapist will work within your comfort level and adjust techniques accordingly. Any discomfort usually subsides quickly after treatment, and the benefits outweigh the temporary discomfort.

Is a doctor's referral required for physiotherapy?

In most countries and states, physiotherapists can provide direct access care without a physician referral. However, some insurance plans may require a doctor's referral for coverage. It's advisable to check with your insurance provider regarding their specific requirements before scheduling an appointment.
